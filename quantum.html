The classical walker flips a fair coin each step: go left or right. Probabilities just add, so outcomes pile into a bell curve that widens like √N — slow, smooth, and centred.

The quantum walker carries a complex amplitude and uses a Hadamard "coin" that puts it in a superposition of left and right every step. Now amplitudes add — and where they meet out of phase they cancel (interference), exactly like the dark fringes of the double slit. The result spreads like N — far faster — and grows two horns instead of a hump.

That single difference — ∂ψ/∂t = i·D·∂²ψ/∂x² instead of ∂P/∂t = D·∂²P/∂x² — is the whole gap between a gambler's diffusion and a quantum particle's interference. The i is everything.
